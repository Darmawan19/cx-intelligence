## Executive Summary

This report analyzes 522 Google Play Store reviews for Shopee Indonesia (com.shopee.id) submitted between May 7–12, 2026, with 77.8% classified as negative and an overall average severity of 2.65 out of 5.0.

The single largest driver of user dissatisfaction is Delivery and Logistics, surfacing in 150 reviews at a severity of 3.17 — the highest in this dataset. Users are directly attributing frustration to late deliveries, unresolved courier issues, and poor order tracking visibility. At a priority score of 675.92, this area outpaces the next issue by more than 2.5 times, meaning it is not just common but acutely painful. Left unaddressed, this will continue to erode post-purchase trust and drive churn at the transaction completion stage — the most costly point in the funnel to lose a customer.

App Performance ranks second with 82 affected reviews and a severity of 2.21. Users report crashes, slow load times, and login failures. While severity is comparatively lower, the volume and the nature of the issue are critical: app instability creates friction before a transaction even begins, suppressing conversion and disproportionately affecting new or returning users attempting re-engagement. A degraded app experience amplifies perception of every other problem in this report.

Checkout and Payment issues appear in 65 reviews at a severity of 2.74, with users citing failed transactions, payment method errors, and voucher application breakdowns. Although third in volume, the severity score sits above App Performance, indicating that when these failures occur, they land harder. Payment failures at the final step represent direct, measurable revenue loss per incident.

Prioritization follows a composite logic of volume, severity, and business impact. Delivery leads because it combines scale, high severity, and downstream trust damage. App Performance follows because it is a prerequisite to all other user journeys. Payment issues close the top three due to their direct and quantifiable revenue consequence.

This analysis draws on 522 verified user reviews over a six-day window from a single high-signal source, providing sufficient volume and recency for confident directional conclusions.

## DEEP DIVE: Delivery & Logistics

### 1. Issue Summary
Users are experiencing widespread delivery failures across three dimensions: packages stuck in transit for days or weeks beyond the estimated delivery date, packages marked as delivered but never received (including suspected theft by couriers), and couriers returning packages without attempting delivery or contacting recipients. SPX Express is the most frequently named culprit, and Shopee's customer service is widely criticised for failing to resolve complaints meaningfully.

### 5. Root Cause Hypothesis

The complaints cluster around **two compounding root causes**:

**(c) Policy/business problem — primary root cause:**
Shopee's shift toward owning its logistics layer (SPX Express) has outpaced its operational capacity. SPX hub infrastructure appears under-resourced in key transit nodes (Bekasi, Bogor, Tangerang), creating systemic bottlenecks. More critically, there is **no enforceable delivery accountability mechanism** for SPX couriers: no mandatory photo/WA proof of delivery, no public courier identity attached to each delivery, and no real escalation path when a courier falsifies status. The recent reduction in third-party courier partnerships (JNT, JNE mentioned by users) has removed competitive redundancy, leaving users captive to an underperforming network.

**(b) Technical/engineering problem — contributing cause:**
SPX's routing algorithm and GPS mapping system contain errors causing packages to be sent to the wrong city/province and couriers to navigate to incorrect coordinates. This is not random human error — multiple users across different regions describe the same pattern of geographic misrouting, pointing to a systemic data or geocoding issue in the dispatch system.

**(d) Communication/expectation gap — amplifying cause:**
Delivery ETAs are surfaced to users but not reliably maintained or proactively updated when delays occur. Complaint tickets are auto-closed without resolution, and CS is AI-gated. Users discover the failure themselves rather than being informed, significantly amplifying frustration and distrust.

---

### 6. Recommended Action

**Primary Recommendation: Mandate and enforce SPX delivery verification with real-time proof-of-delivery (POD) and introduce courier accountability transparency**

Require all SPX couriers to submit a timestamped photo and optional OTP/WA confirmation at the point of delivery before the system can mark a package as "terkirim." Simultaneously, surface the assigned courier's name and contact number to buyers upon out-for-delivery status (as competing platforms already do). Internally, flag couriers with high return/failed-delivery rates for immediate audit.

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Target user segment** | All SPX Express users, with priority on buyers in high-complaint nodes (Bekasi, Bogor, Tangerang, Lampung, Kalimantan) |
| **Expected impact** | **High** — directly addresses the highest-severity sub-issue (undelivered/stolen packages) and the fake-return problem simultaneously; reduces refund disputes and churn signals |
| **Implementation complexity** | **Medium** — POD photo capture already exists in some courier apps; requires policy enforcement, courier app update, and ops SOP change rather than full rebuild |
| **Suggested owner team** | SPX Logistics Operations (primary), Courier Product Engineering (POD feature), CX Policy & Trust (courier audit framework) |

Users are experiencing a significant degradation in app usability across three interconnected fronts: aggressive third-party ads that forcibly redirect users into the Shopee app from other applications, sluggish and laggy performance especially after recent updates, and recurring bugs and crashes affecting core features. The frustration is compounded by the perception that frequent updates are making things worse, not better, eroding long-term user loyalty.

### 5. Root Cause Hypothesis

The dominant complaint — forced redirects via in-app ads in third-party apps — is primarily a **(c) Policy/business problem**. The ad network is optimizing aggressively for click-through and app-open metrics, deploying deceptive UX patterns (fake close buttons, touch-area manipulation) that technically inflate engagement KPIs while destroying user trust. This appears to be a deliberate growth tactic prioritizing acquisition volume over experience quality.

The secondary issue — app lag, overheating, and bloat — is a **(b) Technical/engineering problem**. The addition of heavyweight features (Shopee Video, live streaming, AI chat, SPayLater) without corresponding performance optimization has ballooned the app's resource footprint. Updates are shipped frequently but appear to add features without resolving underlying memory and CPU inefficiency, particularly for Android devices below mid-range specs — which represent a significant portion of the SEA user base.

The spontaneous app-opening behavior (without ad interaction) suggests a **(b) Technical/engineering problem** at the OS integration layer, likely related to background service or deep-link handling that is not being properly scoped or restricted.

There is also a **(d) Communication/expectation gap**: users receive no changelog with updates, so frequent updates are perceived as chaos rather than care, accelerating trust erosion.

### 6. Recommended Action

**Primary Recommendation: Overhaul the third-party ad redirect experience — immediately restrict forced app-opening behavior and fix deceptive close button mechanics.**

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Target user segment** | All active users on Android who use other high-session apps (Wattpad, mobile games, browsers) — estimated majority of casual shoppers |
| **Expected impact** | **High** — this single sub-issue drives the largest complaint volume (32/82 reviews) and is the leading uninstall trigger mentioned explicitly in reviews |
| **Implementation complexity** | **Medium** — requires ad policy change (enforceable via contract with ad network partners) and a technical fix to close-button touch targets; no core app rebuild needed |
| **Suggested owner team** | Growth/Marketing (policy decision) + Mobile Engineering (technical fix) with CX escalation authority to enforce SLA |

### 5. Root Cause Hypothesis

There are likely **two compounding root causes** operating simultaneously:

**(b) Technical/Engineering Problem** — The payment infrastructure (QRIS reconciliation, ShopeePay ledger, PayLater app module) has reliability gaps: transactions are processed on the payment side but fail to write to order history. This suggests weak transactional atomicity and inadequate payment-state synchronization between services. The QRIS silent reversal issue in particular points to a missing failure-state notification layer.

**(c) Policy/Business Problem** — The COD and PayLater restriction logic is a rule-based risk system that is triggering too broadly and too permanently. Users with no delinquency, or single minor infractions (e.g., one cancellation due to travel), are being locked out for 50+ days with no clear appeal path. The policy appears designed to protect against abuse (fake orders, defaults) but the threshold calibration is miscalibrated, catching legitimate users in the net. The fee transparency issue (biaya ukuran khusus, admin fee disclosed only at checkout) is a **communication/expectation gap** amplified by a business decision to add fees post-browse.

### 6. Recommended Action

**Primary Recommendation: Overhaul COD & PayLater restriction logic with transparent, tiered reinstatement pathways**

Users being silently and indefinitely locked out of COD or PayLater is the highest-volume, cross-cutting issue and is directly causing basket abandonment and platform churn (multiple users explicitly threatening to switch to Tokopedia/TikTok Shop).

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Target User Segment** | Existing buyers with COD or PayLater restrictions, particularly those with no active delinquency (cleared bills, no open disputes) |
| **Expected Impact** | **High** — restoring payment access directly recovers lost GMV from users who self-report they have stopped purchasing |
| **Implementation Complexity** | **Medium** — requires risk policy recalibration (not a full rebuild), a clear in-app reinstatement status UI, and CS escalation tooling |
| **Suggested Owner Team** | Risk & Trust Policy (lead), Payments Product (UI/UX), Customer Experience (CS tooling support) |
